PRD Generator

Turn a feature idea into a structured Product Requirements Document.

How this works
Claude is given a system prompt that instructs it to act as a senior PM and return a PRD with a fixed structure (problem statement, goals, non-goals, user stories, success metrics, open questions). Constraining the output format ensures consistency and makes the output immediately usable — rather than a freeform essay that needs reformatting before sharing.

What I chose and why

  • Streaming over batched. The PRD renders section-by-section as Claude writes — waiting on a full response breaks the iteration loop.
  • Structured sections, not prose. Fixed slots (problem, goals, user stories, metrics) hand off cleanly to eng. A wall of text always needs reformatting before sharing.
  • One-line input, full PRD out. No 10-field form. When the idea is still fuzzy, the friction of a long form kills iteration speed.
