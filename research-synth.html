How this works
Claude is prompted to act as an analyst, not a summarizer. The difference matters: summarization just condenses what's there, while analysis identifies patterns, weighs frequency, and surfaces opportunity areas that aren't explicitly stated in any single response. The structured output format (themes, pain points, opportunities, gaps) maps directly to the artifacts a PM needs before a discovery readout.

Themes

1. Schedule confidence is fragile

Across 12 of 18 interviews, users described checking the payment screen multiple times to "make sure the date didn't shift." The mental model is "if I don't actively verify, the schedule might silently change."

2. Failure recovery is opaque

When a scheduled payment fails, users described being unable to tell whether the system would retry automatically or whether they needed to manually re-initiate. This led to duplicate payments in 3 cases.

Top opportunities

  • Build trust through visible state — "no changes since last view" affirmation
  • Make retry behavior explicit and configurable per scheduled payment
  • Surface failure causes inline, not via separate notification flow
